## Why This Site?

I've always believed that engineers should share their stories — not just their code. After years of building ADAS systems, debugging OTA updates at 2am, and navigating the complex world of autonomous vehicle development, I decided it was time to create a space that goes beyond a traditional resume.

This site is part portfolio, part blog, and part creative outlet.

## What to Expect

Here's what I plan to write about:

- **Automotive Technology** — Deep-dives into ADAS, surround view camera systems, Blue Cruise, and the engineering challenges behind making cars smarter and safer.
- **AI & Computer Vision** — Reflections from my Master's program at the University of Michigan, where I'm studying the intersection of computer vision and autonomous systems.
- **Personal Stories** — From growing up internationally (Netherlands, Idaho, Michigan) to my experience as a UWC scholar, there are stories I want to share that don't fit on a resume.
- **Beyond Engineering** — Thoughts on the automotive industry's future, tech culture, and whatever else catches my attention.

## A Bit About Me

I'm Ahmed — an ADAS Vision and Camera Engineer at Ford Motor Company. My day-to-day involves leading the development and validation of surround view camera systems. Before that, I spent years integrating ADAS features, managing prototype fleets, and building internal tools to make our teams more efficient.

Outside of work, I'm pursuing my Master's in AI with a focus on Computer Vision at the University of Michigan. I'm fascinated by how machines learn to see and understand the world around them.

I'm also a firm believer in giving back. Whether it's volunteering as a coding instructor in Metro Detroit schools or working on projects that address access to clean water, I try to use my skills for good.
